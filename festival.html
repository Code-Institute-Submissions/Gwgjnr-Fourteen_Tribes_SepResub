Festivals

Galway International Arts Festival

The Galway International Arts Festival was founded in 1978 by a group of students from NUIG with a Â£1000 budget, it now plays host to performers such as The Kaiser Chiefs, The Academic and Sinead O'Connor. It also produces unique artwork that tours internationally. The festival maintains non-profit status and ensures at least 25% of the festival programme is free for the public.

Galway International Oyster and Seafood Festival

The Galway International Oyster Festival is held on the on the last weekend of September as this is officially the first month of oyster season. The main event is the World Oyster Opening Championship but you can also find silent discos, family activities and seafood trails. From humble beginnings with 34 attendees, this festival has been was described by the Sunday Times as "one of the 12 greatest shows on earth".

Galway Races

This 7 day event attracts over 150,000 people a year to one of the largest horse-racing events in the world. The highlight of the whole event has to be Ladie's day which is held on Thursday. Each year, fantastic prizes are offered for the title of Best Dressed Lady or Most Elegant Hat. Make sure to keep the last Monday of July every year free as this not one to miss.
